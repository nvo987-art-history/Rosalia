ROSALÍA CODE

The Theory of Perception

Rhythm • Attention • Meaning

Introduction

The Rosalía Code emerged from the observation of a contemporary group choreography performance by Spanish artist Rosalía.

The performance raised a fundamental question.

Why do certain visual experiences feel alive even when no individual movement appears to dominate the composition?

The investigation led beyond contemporary performance and into the work of Sonia Delaunay, one of the pioneers of modern abstraction.

Although separated by more than a century and working in entirely different media, both appeared to solve the same perceptual problem.

How can movement emerge without directly depicting movement itself?

Core Thesis

Meaning is an effect produced by the organization of perception.

The Starting Point

Rosalía Performance

The theory originates from the analysis of a large-scale group choreography performed by Rosalía.

Rather than focusing on narrative, the performance organizes attention through collective movement, rhythm, repetition, and changing spatial relationships.

The Song

The choreography associated with the performance serves as the empirical foundation of the study.

The movement of bodies functions as a perceptual structure rather than a simple sequence of dance actions.

Observation

The viewer's attention continuously shifts across the stage.

Meaning emerges from the movement of attention itself rather than from any isolated performer.

Sonia Delaunay

At the beginning of the twentieth century, Sonia Delaunay developed a radical understanding of visual perception.

Colors do not exist independently.

They interact. They create tension. They generate rhythm.

The viewer's eye moves continuously between visual elements.

Rhythm is not the subject of the image.

Rhythm is the structure of the image itself.

The Comparative Observation

Delaunay

Color fields interact.

The eye moves.

Attention circulates throughout the composition.

Rosalía

Groups of bodies interact.

The gaze moves.

Attention circulates throughout the choreography.

Shared Principle

Meaning does not reside in individual elements.

Meaning emerges from the relationships between them.

Attention Choreography

Classical composition often relies on a central figure.

A hierarchy. A focal point. A stable center.

Both Delaunay's paintings and Rosalía's choreography operate differently.

Attention constantly moves.

The center appears and disappears.

Relationships continuously reorganize themselves.

The viewer is guided through a structured perceptual journey.

Foundational Principles

Principle I

Attention creates structure.

Principle II

Rhythm directs attention.

Principle III

Meaning emerges from relationships, not isolated elements.

Principle IV

The viewer participates in the creation of meaning.

Conclusion

Rosalía's choreography and Sonia Delaunay's visual compositions reveal a common perceptual mechanism.

Neither derives meaning primarily from individual elements.

Meaning emerges through rhythm, relationships, and the organization of attention.

The Rosalía Code proposes that perception itself is an active system.

Meaning is not discovered.

Meaning is produced.

"Meaning is an effect produced by the organization of perception."

ROSALÍA CODE

The Rosalía Code is an ongoing research project examining rhythm, attention, perception, and the emergence of meaning through organized perceptual structures.
